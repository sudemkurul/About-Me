About Me

I am a first-year undergraduate student of Political Science and Public Administration at Istanbul Medipol University, with a profound academic interest in political theory, legal studies, history, and cultural anthropology. My research focuses on how political systems, legal frameworks, and social structures interact to shape governance and societal dynamics.

I complement my studies with independent research and rigorous online courses from leading institutions including Cambridge, Edinburgh, Yale, and Harvard. These engagements deepen my expertise in comparative politics, legal philosophy, public policy, and the interdisciplinary study of governance.

1) Research Interests & Projects

I develop independent research projects and online platforms where I publish essays, book analyses, and articles on history, politics, and law—aiming to communicate complex ideas to a broader audience.

2) Academic Engagements & Achievements

  • Conferences on political science, constitutional law, and governance at Istanbul Medipol University.
  • Panel discussions on human rights, democratic systems, and constitutional reforms.
  • Research essay integrating perspectives from history, anthropology, and political theory.
  • Debates and presentations on international governance, global security, and institutional policymaking.

3) Volunteering & Civic Engagement

  • UNICEF Youth Network — Animal-rights signatures, environmental and social awareness campaigns.
  • TEMA Foundation — Environmental projects and tree-planting activities.
  • Turkish Grand National Assembly (TBMM) Visit — Observed parliamentary processes.
  • 15 July Democracy Rallies — Events commemorating democratic resilience and civic solidarity.

4) Future Plans & Professional Goals

  • National Geographic Volunteer Program (upcoming) — Sustainability and education initiatives.
  • The Royal Historical Society (UK) (planned) — Research publication and networking.
  • British Political Students Association (planned) — Governance and public policy dialogue.
  • European Council on Foreign Relations (ECFR) (planned) — Policy-oriented research and strategy.

5) Vision & Academic Aspirations

My long-term objective is to build an interdisciplinary foundation spanning politics, history, law, and social anthropology, contributing to global research on governance, human rights, international relations, and policy innovation.
