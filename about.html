About Me

I am a first-year undergraduate student of Political Science and Public Administration at Istanbul Medipol University, with a profound academic interest in political theory, legal studies, history, and cultural anthropology. My research focuses on understanding how political systems, legal frameworks, and social structures interact to shape governance and societal dynamics across diverse contexts.

Over the past few years, I have complemented my university studies with extensive independent research and rigorous online courses delivered by leading global institutions, including Cambridge, Edinburgh, Yale, and Harvard. These academic engagements have deepened my expertise in comparative politics, legal philosophy, public policy, and the interdisciplinary study of governance.

1) Research Interests & Projects

Alongside my coursework, I am actively developing a series of independent research projects and online platforms where I publish essays, book analyses, and articles on history, politics, and law. These initiatives enable me to critically examine complex ideas and communicate my findings to a broader audience.

2) Academic Engagements & Achievements

  • Attended high-level conferences on political science, constitutional law, and governance at Istanbul Medipol University.
  • Participated in panel discussions on human rights, democratic systems, and constitutional reforms.
  • Authored an extensive research essay integrating perspectives from history, anthropology, and political theory.
  • Engaged in academic debates and presentations on international governance, global security, and institutional policymaking.

3) Volunteering & Civic Engagement

  • UNICEF Youth Network — Contributed to campaigns focused on animal rights, environmental advocacy, and public awareness.
  • TEMA Foundation — Actively participated in environmental restoration and tree-planting initiatives.
  • Turkish Grand National Assembly (TBMM) Visit — Observed parliamentary proceedings to gain insights into legislative mechanisms.
  • 15 July Democracy Rallies — Took part in events commemorating democratic resilience and civic solidarity.

4) Future Plans & Professional Goals

  • National Geographic Volunteer Program (upcoming) — Contributing to sustainability-focused projects and public education initiatives.
  • The Royal Historical Society (UK) (planned) — Publishing research and connecting with academic historians worldwide.
  • British Political Students Association (planned) — Collaborating on dialogues surrounding governance and public policy.
  • European Council on Foreign Relations (ECFR) (planned) — Participating in policy-oriented research and strategic discussions.

5) Vision & Academic Aspirations

My long-term academic objective is to build a strong interdisciplinary foundation that bridges politics, history, law, and social anthropology. I aspire to contribute to global research on governance, human rights, international relations, and policy innovation, while continuing to publish my findings through writing, conferences, and collaborative initiatives.
